10 FAQ ABOUT NATIONAL PARKS

1. What are three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in Yosemite National Park in California?
Sentinel Fall, Ribbon Fall, and Bridalveil Fall.

2. One of the hottest temperature on earth was recorded in 1913 in Death Valley National Park, registering at?
134 degrees.

3. Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, Alaska, is the largest national park, covering how many square mile?
13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.

4. What is Arches National Park famous for?
Arches National Park is famous for having the largest concentration of stone arches in the world.

5. Which Fall in Yosemite is nine times taller than Niagara Falls?
Ribbon Falls.

6. What is Dry Tortugas National Park in Florida known for?
Dry Tortugas National Park in Florida is known for its world-renowned concentration of turtles.

7. Mount McKinley in the Alaska Range of Denali National Park is the highest point in the national parks at?
20,302 feet.

8. Mesa Verde National Park was the first national park to be recognized for "works of man," and acknowledgement of the incredible cliff dwellings left behind by?
the Pueblo Indians.

9. Carter Lake National Park in Oregon is the deepest lake in the U.S. by how many feet deep?
At 1,932 feet deep.

10. National Park in Arizona, is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World?
The Grand Canyon, in the Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona.
