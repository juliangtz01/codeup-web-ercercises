10 FAQ ABOUT NATIONAL PARKS
1. Yosemite National Park
Three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in Yosemite National Park in California.

2. Death Valley National Park
One of the hottest temperature on earth was recorded in 1913 in Death Valley National Park, registering 134 degrees.

3. Wrangell-St. Elias National Park
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, Alaska, is the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.

4. Arches National Park
Arches National Park is famous for having the largest concentration of stone arches in the world.

5. Utah's National Parks
Utah's five national parks are all within easy driving distance of each other, which means that you can cross off five national parks form your "bucket list" on just one trip.

6. Dry Tortugas National Park
Dry Tortugas National Park in Florida is known for its world-renowned concentration of turtles.

7. Rocky Mountain National Park
Trail Ridge Road in Rocky Mountain National Park, which connects Grand Lake and Estes Park, is the highest continuously paved road in the U.S., and it includes breathtaking views and vistas along its entire 48 miles.

8. Mesa Verde National Park
Mesa Verde National Park was the first national park to be recognized for "works of man," and acknowledgement of the incredible cliff dwellings left behind by the Pueblo Indians.

9. Carter Lake National Park
At 1,932 feet deep, Carter Lake National Park in Oregon is the deepest lake in the U.S.

10. Grand Canyon National Park
The Grand Canyon, in the Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona, is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
